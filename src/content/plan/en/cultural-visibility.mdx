## The Challenge

Gateway is already one of Oregon's most culturally rich neighborhoods. Walk down NE Halsey or Stark Street and you'll pass signs in Vietnamese, Russian, Spanish, Chinese, Somali, and a dozen other languages. Informal economies, cross-cultural networks, and multilingual storefronts make this area uniquely vibrant.

But this cultural richness is largely invisible to formal systems. City planning documents describe the area in terms of deficits — poverty rates, crime statistics, infrastructure gaps — rather than assets. The multicultural character of Gateway is treated as something to manage rather than something to celebrate and support.

<DataPoint source="Portland Bureau of Planning, Community Profile 2025">
Gateway's 97220 ZIP code contains residents from over 80 countries of origin, with 40+ languages spoken at home. Yet fewer than 5% of city-funded public art installations in East Portland reflect this diversity.
</DataPoint>

## Goals

1. **Multilingual public wayfinding and signage** throughout the Gateway district
2. **Public art program** with at least 10 permanent installations reflecting neighborhood cultures
3. **Annual multicultural festival** drawing 5,000+ attendees
4. **Cultural heritage archive** documenting Gateway's immigrant community stories

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Regional Arts & Culture Council", "IRCO", "Community organizations"]}>
**Gateway Cultural Arts Program**

Commission public art installations that reflect the actual cultures of Gateway — not generic civic art, but work created by and with immigrant communities. Murals, sculptures, and installations at transit stops, in commercial corridors, and at community gathering points. Every piece bilingual at minimum.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Portland Bureau of Transportation", "TriMet"]}>
**Multilingual Wayfinding System**

Design and install a comprehensive multilingual wayfinding system throughout Gateway. Transit stops, commercial corridors, parks, and public facilities all receive signage in the five primary community languages. Make navigation intuitive for everyone, regardless of English proficiency.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Community organizations", "Portland State University", "Local schools"]}>
**Gateway Heritage Archive**

Launch a community-driven oral history and archival project documenting the stories of Gateway's immigrant communities. Partner with schools for youth-led interviews. Create a digital archive accessible through the Gateway Confluence platform. Preserve the neighborhood's history as it's being made.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Gateway Area Business Association", "Cultural organizations"]}>
**Gateway Cultures Festival**

Organize an annual multicultural festival celebrating Gateway's diversity through food, music, dance, storytelling, and art. Not a tourist event — a neighborhood celebration. Vendor space prioritized for local businesses. Free admission. Multilingual programming.
</Strategy>
