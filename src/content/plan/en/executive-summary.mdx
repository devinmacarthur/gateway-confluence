## Gateway Confluence: A Community Vision

Gateway Confluence is a strategic framework for community-driven development in East Portland's Gateway district. It is not a plan for revitalization. It is not a speculative growth engine. It is a plan for **staying** — for the people who are already here.

<CommunityVoice attribution="Gateway resident">
We don't need someone to come fix our neighborhood. We need the tools and the power to build what we already know we need.
</CommunityVoice>

## Geographic Scope

The Gateway district sits at the intersection of I-205, the MAX Blue Line, and Burnside Street in East Portland. Centered around 1708 NE 106th Avenue, this plan encompasses the transit-adjacent neighborhoods where Portland's greatest cultural diversity, youngest population, and most significant affordability pressures converge.

## Who This Plan Serves

- **Longtime renters** who face rising costs but have deep neighborhood roots
- **Immigrant families** who have built businesses, social networks, and cultural institutions
- **Youth** who deserve to imagine a local future
- **Small business owners** operating on thin margins in a challenging commercial landscape
- **Elders** who need accessible services and connection
- **All residents** who want infrastructure that serves daily life

## Core Values

1. **Anti-displacement**: Development must not displace existing residents or businesses
2. **Cultural plurality**: The neighborhood's multilingual, multicultural character is a strength to amplify, not a problem to solve
3. **Transit-centered equity**: Proximity to transit should mean proximity to opportunity, not vulnerability to speculation
4. **Community governance**: Residents make decisions about their own neighborhood
5. **Infrastructure as dignity**: Childcare, food access, cooling centers, and workforce training are not amenities — they are rights

## Seven Strategic Pillars

This plan is organized around seven interconnected pillars, each addressing a core dimension of community life in Gateway:

1. **Housing Security** — Zero net displacement, community land trust, tenant protections
2. **Small Business Ecosystem** — Multilingual support, micro-grants, affordable commercial space
3. **Public Space Activation** — Safe crossings, plazas, green buffers, youth spaces
4. **Community Safety** — Lighting, ambassadors, trauma-informed outreach
5. **Institutional Coherence** — Shared identity, governance mechanisms, coalition building
6. **Cultural Visibility** — Multilingual signage, cross-cultural programming, heritage preservation
7. **Shared Infrastructure** — Childcare, workforce training, food access, mental health, climate resilience
