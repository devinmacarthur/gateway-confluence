## The Challenge

East Portland's Gateway neighborhood faces a paradox: it remains one of Portland's more affordable areas, yet housing insecurity is pervasive. Rents have risen steadily. Many apartment buildings are aging. Families double up. And the very transit access that defines Gateway makes it a target for speculative development.

<DataPoint source="Portland Housing Bureau, 2025">
Over 52% of Gateway renters are cost-burdened, spending more than 30% of their income on housing. Nearly 25% are severely cost-burdened, exceeding 50%.
</DataPoint>

The goal is not to attract new development at any cost. The goal is **zero net displacement** of existing residents during any development that occurs.

## Goals

1. **Zero net displacement** of existing residents during neighborhood development
2. **Community land trust** holding at least 200 permanently affordable units by 2032
3. **Right of first refusal** policy for existing tenants when buildings are sold or redeveloped
4. **Preservation of 1,000+ existing affordable units** through building upgrades and tenant protections

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Portland Community Reinvestment Initiatives", "Hacienda CDC"]}>
**Establish the Gateway Community Land Trust (GCLT)**

Launch a community land trust to acquire and permanently steward affordable housing in the Gateway district. Initial capitalization through a combination of TIF funds, philanthropic investment, and public grants. Target: 50 units in the first two years.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Oregon Law Center", "Community Alliance of Tenants"]}>
**Tenant Protection and Right of Return**

Advocate for and implement tenant protection policies including: mandatory relocation assistance, right of first refusal when buildings sell, no-fault eviction protections, and a right-of-return guarantee for displaced tenants.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 2-3" partners={["Home Forward", "Metro Regional Government"]}>
**Existing Building Preservation Program**

Partner with existing apartment owners to upgrade aging buildings without displacing tenants. Offer low-interest rehabilitation loans conditioned on long-term affordability commitments. Target: 500 units preserved in three years.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1-3" partners={["Urban League of Portland", "IRCO"]}>
**Pathways to Stability Program**

Create holistic housing stability services including rent assistance navigation, financial literacy in multiple languages, tenant rights education, and emergency stabilization funds for families facing displacement.
</Strategy>

## What This Looks Like

<CommunityVoice attribution="Gateway apartment resident, 12 years">
I've watched three families on my floor leave in two years. Not because they wanted to — because they couldn't keep up. If someone had just helped with one month's rent, or fought the rent increase, they'd still be here.
</CommunityVoice>

Housing security in Gateway means:
- A family of four in a two-bedroom apartment on NE Halsey paying no more than 30% of their income in rent
- An aging building getting new windows, a new roof, and updated safety systems — without a single tenant being displaced
- A Vietnamese grandmother who has lived in Gateway for 20 years knowing she will never be forced to move
- Young adults who grew up here being able to find their first apartment in the same neighborhood
