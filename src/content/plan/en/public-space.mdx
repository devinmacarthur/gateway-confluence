## The Challenge

Gateway has wide roads, large parking lots, and leftover spaces — but little cohesive civic realm. The district was built for cars, not for people. Transit infrastructure exists, but the spaces between transit stops and homes feel hostile, disconnected, and unsafe.

<DataPoint source="Portland Parks & Recreation Equity Analysis, 2025">
East Portland has 40% less park acreage per capita than inner Portland neighborhoods. Gateway ranks in the bottom 15% citywide for access to quality public space.
</DataPoint>

Public space in Gateway is not just an amenity deficit — it is a civic participation deficit. Without gathering spaces, the community cannot cohere. Without safe corridors, families cannot access services. Without shade and green space, summer heat events become dangerous.

## Goals

1. **Three new activated public plazas** near transit nodes by 2030
2. **Safe pedestrian corridors** connecting the 20 largest apartment complexes to MAX stations and bus stops
3. **Youth-designed spaces** in at least 2 locations
4. **Green buffers** along 102nd Avenue and Burnside Street reducing heat island effect by measurable degree

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Portland Bureau of Transportation", "TriMet", "Metro"]}>
**Gateway Transit Plaza**

Design and build a welcoming civic plaza adjacent to the Gateway Transit Center. Include seating, shade structures, public art, vendor space, and multilingual wayfinding. Currently the transit center area feels like infrastructure, not community space.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Portland Bureau of Transportation", "ODOT"]}>
**Safe Crossings Initiative**

Prioritize pedestrian safety improvements at the 10 most dangerous intersections in Gateway. Include curb extensions, rapid-flash beacons, median refuge islands, and improved lighting. Focus on routes between apartment complexes and transit.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 2-3" partners={["Portland Parks & Recreation", "Depave", "Verde"]}>
**Green Gateway Corridors**

Create tree-lined walking paths, bioswales, and green buffers along major arterials. Partner with Depave to convert unnecessary pavement to green space. Plant 500 trees in the first phase with community volunteer crews.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Gateway Area Business Association", "Self Enhancement Inc."]}>
**Youth Space Initiative**

Engage youth ages 12-24 in designing two dedicated gathering and recreation spaces. Incorporate basketball courts, covered areas for gathering, WiFi access, and creative expression walls. Locations chosen by youth themselves.
</Strategy>
