## The Challenge

Gateway's commercial corridors — along Stark, Halsey, Glisan, and 102nd — are lined with small businesses that serve the neighborhood's diverse population. Vietnamese pho restaurants, Slavic grocery stores, Mexican taquerias, East African markets, and Chinese bakeries exist alongside auto repair shops, laundromats, and independent pharmacies.

This ecosystem is real but fragile.

<DataPoint source="Prosper Portland Economic Analysis, 2025">
Over 60% of Gateway's small businesses are immigrant-owned, with average annual revenue under $250,000 and razor-thin operating margins averaging 3-5%.
</DataPoint>

These businesses face rising operating costs, limited access to capital, language barriers when navigating permits and regulations, and the constant threat of lease non-renewal as property values shift.

## Goals

1. **Long-term affordable commercial leases** for at least 50 small businesses
2. **Multilingual technical assistance** available in 5+ languages
3. **Micro-grant program** distributing $500K in stabilization funding over 3 years
4. **Cooperative development** supporting at least 3 worker-owned cooperatives

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Prosper Portland", "Mercy Corps NW", "IRCO"]}>
**Multilingual Business Support Hub**

Establish a centralized resource providing business assistance in English, Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, and Russian. Services include permit navigation, bookkeeping support, marketing assistance, and lease review — all free of charge.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Craft3", "Beneficial State Bank", "Prosper Portland"]}>
**Gateway Micro-Grant and Micro-Loan Program**

Launch a grant and loan program specifically for Gateway small businesses. Grants of $2,000-$10,000 for equipment, facade improvements, and operational stabilization. Micro-loans up to $25,000 with favorable terms. Application available in all 5 languages.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 2-3" partners={["Oregon Cooperative Development Center", "SCORE"]}>
**Cooperative Development Incubator**

Support the formation of worker-owned cooperatives, particularly in food service, childcare, and cleaning services. Provide legal structure support, business planning, and initial capitalization assistance.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1-3" partners={["Commercial Property Owners", "Prosper Portland"]}>
**Commercial Lease Stabilization**

Negotiate long-term affordable commercial leases with property owners. Explore community ownership of commercial spaces through the Gateway Community Land Trust. Advocate for commercial rent stabilization policies.
</Strategy>
