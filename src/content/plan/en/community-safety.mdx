## The Challenge

Residents experience real safety concerns in Gateway — property crime, visible substance use, inadequate lighting, and hostile street design. But enforcement-only approaches erode trust, particularly in immigrant communities where contact with law enforcement carries additional risk.

Safety in Gateway must be built through presence, design, trust, and care — not through surveillance and policing alone.

<DataPoint source="Portland Police Bureau, 2025">
East Portland precincts report higher property crime rates than the city average, but surveys show that residents' primary safety concern is pedestrian danger and poor lighting, not violent crime.
</DataPoint>

## Goals

1. **Comprehensive street lighting** upgrades along all primary pedestrian routes
2. **Community ambassador program** with 20 trained, multilingual ambassadors
3. **Trauma-informed outreach teams** operating 7 days a week in Gateway
4. **50% reduction in pedestrian-involved incidents** at priority intersections within 3 years

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Portland Bureau of Transportation", "PGE"]}>
**Gateway Lighting Initiative**

Audit and upgrade street lighting along every primary pedestrian route between apartment complexes, transit stops, schools, and commercial areas. Prioritize underpasses, parking lot perimeters, and the transit center area. Well-lit spaces are safer spaces.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Central City Concern", "IRCO", "El Programa Hispano"]}>
**Community Ambassador Program**

Train and deploy 20 community ambassadors — residents who speak the neighborhood's languages and know its rhythms. Ambassadors provide wayfinding, de-escalation, connection to services, and a consistent friendly presence. Not security guards. Community members.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Multnomah County Health Department", "Central City Concern"]}>
**Trauma-Informed Outreach Expansion**

Expand mobile outreach teams operating in Gateway to 7-day coverage. Teams provide immediate support for individuals experiencing mental health crises, substance use challenges, and homelessness — connecting them to services rather than enforcement.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 2-3" partners={["Gateway Area Business Association", "Portland State University"]}>
**Eyes-on-the-Street Commercial Activation**

Support ground-floor commercial activation along key corridors to increase natural surveillance. Active storefronts, vendor carts, and outdoor seating create the kind of busy, watched streets that feel safe without enforcement.
</Strategy>
