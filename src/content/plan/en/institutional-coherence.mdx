## The Challenge

Gateway sits at the intersection of multiple institutional jurisdictions — TriMet, Metro, Portland city bureaus, Multnomah County, Prosper Portland, and dozens of nonprofit service providers. Tax Increment Financing (TIF) districts, transit agency plans, and city bureau budgets all affect the neighborhood, but they operate on different timelines, with different accountability structures, and often with little coordination.

The result: residents experience institutional fragmentation. A dozen well-meaning agencies working in the same geography, but no shared narrative, no common governance, and no coherent vision that belongs to the community itself.

<DataPoint source="East Portland Action Plan Review, 2025">
More than 15 public agencies and 30 nonprofit organizations operate programs in the Gateway district, yet only 3 have formal coordination agreements with each other.
</DataPoint>

## Goals

1. **Gateway Community Governance Council** — a resident-led body with formal advisory authority
2. **Shared Community Vision Document** — adopted by all major institutional partners
3. **Coordinated investment framework** aligning TIF, transit, and municipal spending
4. **Annual community accountability summit** — public reporting on commitments and progress

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1" partners={["City of Portland", "Metro", "TriMet"]}>
**Establish the Gateway Community Governance Council**

Create a resident-led governance body with formal recognition from the City of Portland. The Council includes elected residents (proportional representation by language community), small business owners, youth representatives, and elder representatives. The Council reviews and advises on all major public investments in the Gateway district.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Prosper Portland", "Portland Bureau of Planning and Sustainability"]}>
**Adopt a Shared Community Vision**

Convene all institutional partners — public agencies, nonprofits, and community organizations — to formally adopt Gateway Confluence's strategic vision as a shared framework. This creates a single reference point for investment decisions, grant applications, and program design.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 2" partners={["Prosper Portland", "Metro", "City Budget Office"]}>
**Coordinated Investment Framework**

Map all current and planned public investments in the Gateway district. Identify overlaps, gaps, and misalignments. Create a coordinated investment plan that sequences projects for maximum community benefit and minimizes disruption.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="medium" timeline="Year 1" partners={["All partners"]}>
**Annual Community Accountability Summit**

Host an annual public event where every institutional partner reports on their commitments to Gateway, progress against benchmarks, and plans for the coming year. Multilingual format with childcare provided. Livestreamed and recorded.
</Strategy>
