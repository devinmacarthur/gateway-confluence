## The Challenge

In much of Portland, "amenities" are marketed as luxury add-ons — rooftop pools, co-working spaces, artisan coffee bars. In Gateway, the infrastructure gap is more fundamental. Families need childcare they can afford. Workers need training they can access. Everyone needs food they can reach without a car. And as summers grow hotter, cooling centers are no longer a convenience — they are a survival necessity.

<DataPoint source="Multnomah County Climate Action Plan, 2025">
East Portland experiences urban heat island temperatures 8-12°F higher than inner Portland during summer heat events. Gateway has the fewest cooling centers per capita of any Portland district.
</DataPoint>

Shared infrastructure is not about amenities. It is about dignity. It is the physical and institutional foundation that makes daily life possible.

## Goals

1. **Affordable childcare access** for 500 additional families
2. **Workforce training center** with multilingual programs serving 1,000 residents annually
3. **Community food hub** connecting local growers, food banks, and families
4. **4 cooling centers and resilience hubs** operational by summer 2028
5. **Multilingual mental health services** available 5 days a week

## Strategies

<Strategy priority="critical" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Multnomah County", "Community childcare providers"]}>
**Gateway Childcare Cooperative Network**

Support the creation of a cooperative network of home-based and center-based childcare providers. Provide business support, licensing assistance, facility upgrades, and a shared waitlist system. Target: 500 new affordable childcare slots in 3 years.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["WorkSystems Inc.", "Portland Community College", "IRCO"]}>
**Gateway Workforce Hub**

Establish a workforce training center offering skills programs aligned with regional job demand — healthcare, construction trades, technology, and food service. All programs available in multiple languages with wraparound services including childcare, transportation, and case management.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-3" partners={["Oregon Food Bank", "Local farms", "Community gardens"]}>
**Community Food Hub**

Create a centralized food distribution and access point connecting Gateway residents with affordable, culturally appropriate food. Include a community kitchen for food business incubation, partnerships with culturally specific farms and gardens, and a mobile distribution program reaching isolated apartment complexes.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="critical" timeline="Year 1" partners={["Multnomah County Emergency Management", "Portland Bureau of Emergency Management"]}>
**Cooling Centers and Resilience Hubs**

Establish 4 permanent resilience hubs that serve as cooling centers in summer, warming centers in winter, and community gathering spaces year-round. Equip with backup power, water, first aid, and multilingual signage. Located at schools, community centers, and places of worship.
</Strategy>

<Strategy priority="high" timeline="Year 1-2" partners={["Cascadia Behavioral Healthcare", "NAMI Multnomah", "IRCO"]}>
**Multilingual Mental Health Initiative**

Launch a community mental health program with counselors who speak Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, Russian, and Somali. Services include individual counseling, family therapy, youth support groups, and crisis intervention. Reduce stigma through community education campaigns in multiple languages.
</Strategy>
