# Gateway Confluence
## A Community Strategic Plan for East Portland

**Location:** Gateway District, Portland OR 97220
**Center:** 1708 NE 106th Avenue, Portland, Oregon
**Status:** Draft — Community Review

---

## Who We Are

Gateway Confluence is a community-driven initiative for East Portland's Gateway district. We are building a transit-centered, culturally plural, economically stable neighborhood — where longtime residents can remain, small businesses can thrive without displacement, public space fosters belonging, and infrastructure serves daily life rather than speculative growth.

This is not a revitalization plan. This is a plan for staying. For the people already here.

---

## Our Community

Gateway is one of the most diverse neighborhoods in Oregon:

- **40+ languages** spoken
- **80+ countries** of origin represented
- **50,000+** residents
- **500+** small businesses, 60% immigrant-owned
- **7 strategic pillars** guiding our work

Gateway's multilingual, multicultural character is our greatest strength. We amplify it — we don't manage it.

---

## Our Values

**Anti-Displacement** — Development must not displace existing residents or businesses. Every project strengthens the community that already exists.

**Cultural Plurality** — Gateway's diversity is not a problem to solve. It is a foundation to build on.

**Community Governance** — Residents make decisions about their own neighborhood. Not advisory. Real power.

**Infrastructure as Dignity** — Childcare, food access, cooling centers, and workforce training are rights, not amenities.

**Transit-Centered Equity** — Public transit should connect people to opportunity, not push them out.

## The Seven Strategic Pillars

### 1. Housing Security Without Displacement
*Priority: Critical*

52% of Gateway renters spend more than 30% of their income on rent. 25% spend more than half.

**Goals:**
- Zero net displacement of existing residents
- 200-unit community land trust by 2032
- Right-of-first-refusal policies for tenants
- Preserve 1,000+ existing affordable units

**Key Actions:**
- Establish the Gateway Community Land Trust (GCLT)
- Tenant protection and right-of-return programs
- Existing building preservation — 500 units over 3 years
- Pathways to housing stability for families

---

### 2. Small Business Ecosystem
*Priority: High*

Gateway's small businesses — food carts, repair shops, salons, grocers — are the economic and cultural backbone of the district. Most operate on margins of 3-5%.

**Goals:**
- 50 businesses with stable, long-term leases
- Multilingual business support in 5+ languages
- $500,000 in micro-grants over 3 years
- 3 worker-owned cooperatives launched

**Key Actions:**
- Multilingual business support hub
- Gateway micro-grant and micro-loan program
- Cooperative development incubator
- Commercial lease stabilization advocacy

---

### 3. Public Space Activation
*Priority: High*

East Portland has 40% less park space per person than the rest of the city. Gateway was built for cars, not people.

**Goals:**
- 3 activated public plazas
- Safe pedestrian corridors connecting transit to daily life
- Youth-designed spaces in 2+ locations
- Green buffers along major roads

**Key Actions:**
- Reimagine the Gateway Transit Plaza
- Safe crossings at the 10 most dangerous intersections
- Green gateway corridors — 500 trees in the first phase
- Youth-led space design for ages 12-24

---

### 4. Community Safety
*Priority: High*

When residents talk about safety, they talk about dark streets, dangerous crossings, and isolation — not crime statistics.

**Goals:**
- Comprehensive street lighting throughout the district
- 20 trained multilingual community ambassadors
- Trauma-informed outreach 7 days per week
- 50% reduction in pedestrian injuries

**Key Actions:**
- Gateway lighting initiative
- Community ambassador program
- Trauma-informed outreach expansion
- Eyes-on-the-street commercial activation

---

### 5. Institutional Coherence
*Priority: High*

15+ public agencies and 30+ nonprofits operate in Gateway — but only 3 have formal coordination agreements.

**Goals:**
- Resident-led governance council with formal advisory authority
- Shared community vision adopted by all institutional partners
- Coordinated investment framework
- Annual community accountability summit

**Key Actions:**
- Establish the Gateway Community Governance Council
- Adopt a shared community vision across agencies
- Create a coordinated investment framework
- Host annual accountability summit — residents ask, institutions answer

---

### 6. Cultural Visibility
*Priority: High*

80 countries of origin. 40+ languages. But Gateway's cultural richness is invisible to formal systems.

**Goals:**
- Multilingual wayfinding signage throughout the district
- 10 public art installations by local artists
- Annual multicultural festival — 5,000+ attendees
- Community cultural heritage archive

**Key Actions:**
- Gateway cultural arts program
- Multilingual wayfinding system
- Gateway heritage archive — collecting oral histories
- Gateway Cultures Festival — celebrating every community

---

### 7. Shared Infrastructure
*Priority: Critical*

Gateway has fundamental infrastructure gaps: childcare, workforce training, food access, and climate resilience.

**Goals:**
- Affordable childcare for 500+ families
- Workforce training for 1,000 residents per year
- Community food hub
- 4 cooling and resilience hubs
- Multilingual mental health services

**Key Actions:**
- Gateway childcare cooperative network
- Gateway workforce hub — multilingual job training
- Community food hub — connecting growers, kitchens, and families
- Cooling centers and resilience hubs for extreme heat
- Multilingual mental health initiative

## How to Get Involved

**Volunteer** — Join a working group aligned with one of our seven strategic pillars. We need organizers, translators, researchers, and community connectors.

**Attend a Meeting** — Our monthly community meetings are open to everyone. Interpretation is available in Spanish, Vietnamese, Chinese, and Russian. Childcare is provided.

**Share Your Story** — Your experience shapes this plan. Whether you've lived in Gateway for decades or just moved in, your perspective matters.

**Spread the Word** — Tell your neighbors about Gateway Confluence. Share our website. Invite someone to a meeting. Community power grows one conversation at a time.

---

## Working Groups

Each working group corresponds to a strategic pillar:

1. **Housing Security** — Anti-displacement, tenant rights, community land trust
2. **Small Business** — Micro-grants, cooperative development, lease stabilization
3. **Public Space** — Transit plaza, safe crossings, green corridors
4. **Community Safety** — Lighting, ambassadors, trauma-informed outreach
5. **Governance** — Community governance council, institutional accountability
6. **Cultural Visibility** — Public art, multilingual signage, heritage archive
7. **Shared Infrastructure** — Childcare, workforce, food hub, cooling centers
